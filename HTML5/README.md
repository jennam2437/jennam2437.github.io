## About this Project :accessibility:

This project informs and educates UX designers on how the importance of designing video transitions with accessibility in mind, and how to implement those features. 

### Teamwork :dancers:

Our work was divided amongst team members as such:
* **Jenna:**
    Key concepts to keep in mind when designing transitions and why they matter for accessibility.
* **Mira:**
    Guidance and best practices, tips, and examples of accessible video transitions.
* **Stacy:**
    Step by step "How To" guide on adding transitions and controlling timing in ClipChamp

### Tools 🛠️

This project was initially drafted on Google Docs and later transferred to Madcap Flare. My team used universal design concepts, the social model of disability, and POUR concepts when creating our content. 

### Process Description 💬

My team and I created a Google Doc in which we each drafted our ouwn section on accessible video transitions. We linked our sources at the end of each document. We then read each others sections' and indentified areas where information could be linked to another section or edited for redundancy. After our draft was finalized, we each created MadCap Flare project and inserted our content. 

## Takeaways 📓

3 things I have learned from this project so far: 
1. In terms of content, working with a team can be challenging! When writing my intial draft, I was worried about repeating topics that other members have already covered. It is best to have a clear list of topics designated from the start. 
2. It is easier to draft content in a word processor and then transfer it into a HAT such as MadCap Flare. 
3. Accessibility benefits everyone! Learning about accessible practices in not only video transitions, but in all digital spaces has opened my eyes to an entirely new facet of UX design. While some may think accessibility is targeted towards a very small population, having an accessibile product can benefit every single user.
